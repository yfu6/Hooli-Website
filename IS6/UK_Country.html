What do information systems graduates do?

More than three quarters of information systems graduates are employed six months after graduation. Four of the top five jobs held by graduates are related to IT and include programmers and software developers, IT user-support technicians, IT and telecommunications professionals, and IT business analysts, architects and systems designers. More than a tenth are undertaking further study or combining study and work. This may be to develop an area of expertise or to broaden their career options.

Individuals with an MIS degree can expect to earn anywhere between $50,000 and upwards of $120,000 depending on location, experience, and company size. The average salary for individuals with MIS degrees can vary greatly within this range, however. The median pay for Information Systems Managers as of 2010 was roughly $115, 780 per year with an entry level degree and five years of work experience. In comparison, though, an individual working in data communication or mobile networking may see a salary closer to $69, 000 a year.

Work experience

Getting a range of work experience in hardware, operating systems, software and team leadership will enhance your skills portfolio. Some degree courses include a placement year or a placement in your final year of study, where you can develop your practical skills and build a network of contacts. Summer placements and internships in IT are also offered by a range of companies. Speak to your university careers service about where to find vacancies. Work experience in a corporate environment can help to develop your commercial awareness, client management skills and the ability to understand and communicate the business benefits of IT. Getting student membership of a professional body such as the BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT is also useful for keeping up to date with the latest news, networking opportunities and finding out about the latest jobs. It's also the pathway towards professional membership and chartered IT status.

Salary Variance

From database to management, the opportunities and payouts vary greatly for individuals in the Management Information Systems field. A degree in MIS qualifies an individual for a wide range of occupations including systems analysis, user support, consulting, training, programming, developing as well as others. Just as the opportunities differ, so do the associated salaries. The lower end of the spectrum is seated within the realm of network and computer systems administration, which still renders a sizeable nearly $70,000 per year, while the highest paying positions tend to be in computer systems design, finance, and insurance with individuals in these fields bringing in closer to $120,000.
